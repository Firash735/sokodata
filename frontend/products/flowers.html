The story behind
Kenyan from Kenya.

Lake Naivasha sits in the Rift Valley at 1,884 metres — the perfect altitude for year-round flower production. Stable temperatures of 12–25°C, 12 hours of sunlight daily, and abundant lake water create conditions that produce blooms of exceptional size, vibrant colour, and extraordinary vase life. Kenya supplies 38% of all flowers sold in the European Union.

Compliance & certifications

What you need
to export legally.

Every certification requirement for kenyan cut flowers export — by destination market. Missing any of these will result in shipment rejection at destination.

🌿
KEPHIS Phytosanitary REQUIRED
Per-consignment certificate from Kenya Plant Health Inspectorate Service. Inspected at packhouse before departure.
🏗
AFA / HCD License REQUIRED
Agriculture and Food Authority, Horticultural Crops Directorate. Annual license for all Kenyan flower exporters.
✅
MPS-ABC Certification EU PREFERRED
Sustainable floriculture standard. Required by most Dutch auction buyers and European supermarket chains.
✈️
IATA Perishables REQUIRED
IATA perishable cargo regulations compliance. Required for all air freight of cut flowers.
