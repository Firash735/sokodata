01 — The Problem

Farmers grow the food.
Middlemen know the price.

In East Africa, commodity price information has always been asymmetric. Buyers know what crops are worth on the global market. Farmers find out only after they have already sold — too late to negotiate, too late to hold, too late to act.

📈
Information Asymmetry
A coffee buyer in Kirinyaga knows the NCE auction cleared at KES 637/kg. The farmer selling cherry at the gate does not. That gap is profit extracted from the farmer every single harvest.

avg farmer undervaluation
🚫
No Exploitation Detection
When a farmer is offered 30% below the fair benchmark price, no alarm sounds. No alert fires. No cooperative is notified. The transaction completes and the loss is invisible — and unrecorded.

No Reach to the Farmer

Somali Pastoral Ledger
The untouched market.

Somalia's livestock sector is worth $400M annually in Gulf exports. It runs on clan-based paper records. The government SOMLITS plan is centralized and paper-heavy. Nobody has built the distributed, offline-first version. That is SPL.

Why this has never been built
SOMLITS is centralized and requires consistent internet. Every deployed agri-tech platform in Somalia assumes connectivity. SPL assumes none. It works in Somaliland's highlands, Puntland's pastoral zones, and the Bay region equally well — because it does not need the cloud to function.
The clan network as consensus layer
Clans already function as a distributed trust network. Livestock ownership disputes are resolved by elder consensus — the same mechanism SPL formalizes with cryptographic signatures. We are not replacing the social system. We are adding immutable proof on top of it.
Gulf exporters will pay for this
Saudi Arabia and UAE have rejected East African livestock shipments over disease certification failures. A verified, immutable health and ownership chain solves an active problem that costs Somali exporters millions per year. The buyer is motivated. The price point is established.
The SokoData connection
Every SPL-registered animal links directly to SokoData's marketplace. The herder sees the live Gulf export price before negotiating. The buyer sees the full traceability record before paying. One platform. Complete verified transaction from Somali herder to Dubai buyer.
