The problem

Farmers grow the food.
Middlemen know the price.

In East Africa, commodity price information has always been asymmetric. Buyers know what crops are worth on the global market. Farmers find out only after they have already sold — too late to negotiate, too late to hold, too late to act.

01
Information asymmetry
A coffee buyer in Kirinyaga knows the NCE auction price. The farmer selling cherry at the gate does not. That gap is profit extracted from the farmer every single harvest.
02
No exploitation detection
When a farmer is offered 30% below the fair benchmark price, no alarm sounds. No alert fires. No cooperative is notified. The transaction completes and the loss is invisible.
03
Currency blindness
Global commodity prices are in USD. Farmers transact in KES, UGX, SOS. Without live forex conversion, global benchmarks are meaningless to the people who need them most.

How it works

From field to phone
in under 6 hours.

STEP 01
Data fetched automatically
Every 6 hours, SokoData pulls live prices from KAMIS, FEWS NET, World Bank, ICO, and ExchangeRate-API. Zero manual input. Zero delay.
STEP 02
Converted to local currency
All global benchmarks are instantly converted to KES, SOS, UGX, and TZS using live forex rates. Farmers see prices in the currency they use.
STEP 03
Exploitation check runs
Every local price is compared to its global benchmark. If any price falls below 80% of fair value, an alert is triggered immediately.
STEP 04
Alert reaches the farmer
Exploitation alerts go directly to farming cooperatives via SMS — in English and Kiswahili — before the transaction completes.

About SokoData
"Price information is power. We are putting that power back in the hands of the people who grow Africa's food."

SokoData was built by a team rooted in East Africa — with firsthand understanding of what it means when a Somali pastoralist in Mandera does not know that the same camel sells for 40% more in Burao, or when a Kenyan coffee farmer accepts KES 85/kg because they do not know the NCE auction just cleared at KES 637/kg.

We built SokoData to close that information gap permanently. Not with a report published once a month. With live data, updated every 6 hours, delivered in the language and format that farmers and traders can actually use.
